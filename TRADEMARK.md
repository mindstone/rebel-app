rights in unregistered marks — are separate from copyright and license
rights.** This policy ensures the "Rebel" name continues to refer
unambiguously to the Mindstone-led project, so users can trust what they
are getting.

Nothing in the BSL-1.1 grants any right to use the Rebel Marks. This policy
describes the limited use we permit without further written permission, and
the uses for which written permission is required.

- Use "Rebel" in factual, descriptive statements about your product, service,
  integration, or repository — for example, "Built with Rebel",
  "Rebel-compatible", "Powered by Rebel", or "An MCP server for Rebel" —
  provided the use is truthful, non-misleading, and does not imply endorsement,
  sponsorship, or affiliation by Mindstone.

  branding, and copyright notices.
- Reproduce the Rebel logo at its native resolution and aspect ratio for the

## Forks and derivative projects

If you fork Rebel under the BSL-1.1 and distribute your fork, you **must rename
your fork** so it is not called Rebel and is not confusingly similar to
Rebel. Choose a name that distinguishes your project from upstream while
making the lineage clear in descriptive copy where you wish to do so. For
example, "MyProject (a Rebel fork)" or "MyProject — based on Rebel" used in
descriptive prose is fine; "Rebel-MyEdition" used as the project name is
not.

This requirement applies regardless of how much of the original code remains
in your fork.
